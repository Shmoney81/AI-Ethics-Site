The Threat of AI

AI models are consuming unprecedented amounts of electricity and water. If we continue to prioritize speed over efficiency, the environmental cost will skyrocket, threatening the right to clean air and water for future generations.

Electricity Usage of AI Models

The existing chips used to train and run AI models prioritize speed over efficiency. In 2024, US data centers consumed 183 TWh of electricity, enough to power a small country for a year (Pew, 2025). If AI use continues to scale without efficiency improvements, power grids will be overwhelmed. However, if energy-efficient chips are prioritized, elecricity consumption can be more than halved, even by conservative projections.

Projected electricity consumption as AI scales. Sources: IEA (2023), Patterson et al., Stanford (2021)

Water Usage of AI Models

Projected water consumption as AI scales. Sources: Intel (2023), TSMC (2023), NVIDIA Technical Sheets

In order to cool the processors, data centers use clean, fresh water. In 2023, data centers in the US alone used approximately 17 billion gallons of water, enough to supply about 155,000 households for a year (Pew, 2025). However, that is not the extent of the water impact. Because data centers draw electricity from power grids, which also use fresh water for cooling and power generation, the total water footprint is much larger. By switching to energy-efficient chips, less heat will be produced, and water consumption can be significantly reduced, not only through direct consumption but also indirectly through electricity use.

Ethical Considerations

Due to the massive amount of electricity consumed by data centers, and the fact that most electricity is still generated from fossil fuels, AI contributes significantly to carbon emissions. This means that the rapid scaling of AI has serious ethical implications, not only violating the right to clean water, but also to clean air. Given that energy-efficient chips are predicted to more than halve the electricity consumption of AI models, the carbon emissions will follow suit. Prioritizing efficiency over speed is not just a technical choice, but an ethical imperative.

Estimated carbon emissions per model training. Sources: IEA (2023), NVIDIA, Academic Scaling Studies

Our Solution: Two-Sided AI Tax

Historical impact of environmental taxes on emissions/energy use. Hypothetical projection for AI chip efficiency included. Sources: Sweden Carbon Tax, UK Carbon Pricing.

Without government intervention, chip-makers have no reason to prioritize energy-efficiency over speed. To address this and improve the quality of life for future generations, we propose a two-sided AI tax. By rewarding chip-makers that produce energy-efficient chips and taxing those that produce speed-prioritized chips, we can create a market incentive for efficiency. Historical data from countries that have implemented environmental taxes show significant reductions in emissions and energy use. By applying a similar approach to AI chips, we can drive innovation towards more sustainable technologies, ensuring that the growth of AI does not come at the expense of our planet's health.
